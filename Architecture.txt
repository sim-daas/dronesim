Comprehensive Architecture Specification: 24/7 Autonomous Surveillance & Precision Landing System
1. Field of Architecture
The present architecture relates to an integrated autonomous surveillance ecosystem comprising a multi-drone fleet, a central base station (CBS), and an automated battery-management dock. The system is designed for persistent Intelligence, Surveillance, and Reconnaissance (ISR) through a "Relay Race" handover protocol and high-precision terminal guidance using nested visual fiducials.
2. System Hardware Infrastructure
2.1 The Aerial Platform (UAV)
The UAV is configured as a high-endurance multi-rotor platform optimized for onboard edge intelligence.
* Onboard Processing (Tier 1): NVIDIA Jetson Nano/Orin serving as the "Cognitive Processor." It executes zero-copy video analytics via the NVIDIA DeepStream pipeline and manages the high-level ROS2 Behavior Trees.
* Flight Control (Tier 2): A PX4-based Flight Controller (FC) managing low-level PID loops, EKF2 state estimation, and the uXRCE-DDS middleware client.
* Vision Subsystem: A global-shutter Raspberry Pi camera equipped with a 940nm narrow-bandpass filter to isolate active IR markers from solar noise.
* Navigation & Rangefinding: A multi-constellation GNSS module synchronized via 1-PPS (Pulse Per Second) to the Jetson clock, coupled with a 1D Lidar for precision AGL (Above Ground Level) measurements during landing.
* Payload Deployment: A high-torque, metal-geared servo-actuated mechanism for the controlled release of mission-specific payloads, triggered either by AI threat validation or manual ground command.
2.2 The Ground Docking Station
* Active Marker System: A landing pad featuring "Negative" Fractal STag markers illuminated by 940nm IR LEDs, providing infinite contrast for the vision subsystem.
* Energy Management: An Automated Battery Swapping System (ABSS) or active conductive clamping system with integrated HVAC to maintain battery health within a 0°C to 45°C charging envelope.
3. Software Orchestration & Protocols
3.1 Data Transport Layer
The system utilizes a multi-band communication strategy to ensure redundancy:
* 433/915 MHz: MAVLink telemetry for long-range heartbeats and critical status updates.
* 2.4 GHz: Control commands and mission waypoint synchronization.
* 5 GHz / Wi-Fi Mesh: High-bandwidth FPV video streaming and ROS2 topic sharing between swarm members.
* Internal Bus: High-speed UART (up to 3M Baud) using the uXRCE-DDS protocol for Jetson-to-PX4 communication.
3.2 Vision & AI Pipeline
The vision logic employs Position-Based Visual Servoing (PBVS). The system solves the Perspective-n-Point (PnP) problem to reconstruct a 6-DOF pose relative to the dock.
* Fractal Logic: The system dynamically switches between "Outer Ring" (long-range detection >2.5m) and "Inner Ring" (short-range precision <2.5m) to maintain sub-centimeter resolution.
* Motion Prediction: A localized Kalman Filter predicts the trajectory of the landing pad if mounted on a mobile or relocatable base.
4. Operational Workflow & Mission Lifecycle
4.1 Phase I: Pre-Flight & Weighted-Sum Scheduling Logic
The central orchestration logic is governed by a high-level Weighted-Sum Scheduler (WSS). This sub-system ensures that fleet deployment is not merely based on proximity, but on a multidimensional health and efficiency matrix.
4.1.1 The Priority Selection Matrix
The CBS calculates a Deployment Priority Score ($P_d$) for every available drone in the standby queue. The selection follows the formula:
$$P_d = (\alpha \cdot SOC) + (\beta \cdot SOH) - (\gamma \cdot D_b) - (\delta \cdot R_i)$$
Where:
* $\alpha, \beta, \gamma, \delta$ are weighting coefficients tuned for mission-criticality.
* SOC (State of Charge): Current usable energy percentage.
* SOH (State of Health): A ratio of current full-charge capacity to original capacity, derived from historical cycle data.
* $D_b$ (Distance to Breach): The physical distance from the drone's current docking port to the mission starting waypoint.
* $R_i$ (Internal Resistance): A real-time telemetry value indicating battery degradation; high $R_i$ signals potential voltage sags under high-current maneuvers.
4.1.2 Automatic Scheduling & Reassignment
The scheduler operates as a "live-loop" monitor. If a drone assigned to a mission $(UAV_x)$ reports a sudden drop in $SOH$ or an $R_i$ spike during pre-arm checks, the Validation Gate triggers an Immediate Reassignment Event.
1. Interrupt: The CBS halts the launch sequence of $UAV_x$.
2. Re-Scan: The WSS re-runs the priority matrix against the remaining fleet.
3. Redeploy: The mission parameters (Tiling paths, payload configs) are injected into the next highest-scoring unit $(UAV_y)$.
4. Log: The degraded unit is flagged for "Manual Inspection Required" and moved to the back of the charging queue.
4.1.3 Environmental Adaptive Tiling
The scheduling logic integrates with a C++ Tiling Engine. This engine calculates the total energy cost ($E_{total}$) based on the user-defined polygon area, desired target altitude, and target overlap. If $E_{total} > (SOC_{available} \times Reserve\_Margin)$, the scheduler automatically splits the mission into a Multi-Sortie Operation, assigning different sectors of the polygon to different drones based on their specific health profiles.
4.2 Phase II: Persistent Patrol & Tiling
Drones navigate the mission area using a two-tier avoidance system:
1. Global Layer: Pre-mapped static obstacles in the CBS database.
2. Local Layer: Reactive avoidance using onboard ultrasonic and Lidar sensors (Vector Field Histogram).
The coverage is optimized through "Lawnmower" or "Dense Inspection" patterns, with optional waypoint randomization to prevent surveillance predictability.
4.3 Phase III: The "Relay Race" Handover
To achieve zero-downtime, the system utilizes a vertical buffer strategy:
   * When Drone A reaches the Dynamic RTH Threshold (calculated as Distance * Energy/Meter + 15% Buffer), the CBS launches Drone B.
   * Drone A remains on station until Drone B broadcasts a "Sector Entry" signal via the mesh network.
   * Drones maintain a Vertical Offset (e.g., 10m separation) during the GPS coordinate handover to eliminate collision risk.
4.4 Phase IV: Precision Landing & Docking
Upon reaching the base coordinates, the drone enters "Hunting Mode":
   1. Acquisition: The Jetson identifies the IR STag marker.
   2. Alignment: PBVS aligns the drone’s X/Y axes with the dock center.
   3. Descent: The Lidar provides the primary Z-axis feedback to prevent "Ground Effect" oscillations.
   4. Touchdown: In the final 0.5s, the system enters Blind Dead-Reckoning, relying on high-frequency IMU integration to maintain attitude during the physical contact phase.
5. Emergency Fail-Safes
   * Semantic "Safe-Surface" Landing: In the event of critical battery or total signal loss, the Jetson runs a semantic segmentation model to identify grass or pavement for emergency landing, avoiding roads, people, or water.
   * Virtual Landing Queue: If multiple drones return simultaneously, the CBS assigns them to a "Vertical Stack" (Wait Altitudes), clearing them for landing sequentially only after the dock confirms a "Ready/Vacant" state.
